**BSc · Instructor**

This module equips students with the essential mathematical techniques required to analyze and interpret data 
effectively in modern business and finance. Designed as a critical quantitative stepping stone, the course provides the 
necessary mathematical scaffolding to ensure success in upcoming, advanced modules such as programming and econometrics. Students 
will develop a robust command of fundamental tools, spanning logic and set theory, differentiation, integration, and 
linear algebra—including matrix operations and vector spaces.

Rather than treating these concepts in isolation, the module emphasizes their practical execution. Students will actively apply 
optimization techniques to solve both constrained and unconstrained real-world problems, bridging abstract theory with concrete 
financial and corporate data analytics. By the conclusion of the course, participants will possess the analytical mindset and 
mathematical fluency needed to tackle complex, data-driven challenges in the global business landscape.

This module covers essential mathematical  techniques and concepts necessary for the effective  analysis of data in business 
and finance and thus facilitate their understanding of modules they see in the next terms (such as those in programming or
econometrics). The module introduces fundamental mathematical tools, including logic and set theory,  differentiation, 
integration, optimisation, and linear algebra. Students explore key topics, such as matrix operations and vector spaces, 
alongside practical applications in finance and data analytics. Additionally, the module focuses on optimisation
techniques for both constrained and unconstrained problems. Throughout the course, students learn to apply these mathematical 
methods to solve real-world problems in business and finance.
The aims of this module are to:
- Introduce students to essential mathematical techniques and concepts needed for the effective analysis of data in business and finance,
- Explore fundamental mathematical tools used in data-driven decision-making, including logic, differentiation, integration, optimisation, and linear algebra,
- Cover key aspects of calculus, such as differentiation and integration, with a focus on their practical use in business modelling and financial analysis,
- Develop problem-solving skills through optimization techniques, including both unconstrained and constrained optimization, and
- Equip students with the mathematical tools to apply these techniques to real-world problems in data analytics, business, and finance.
